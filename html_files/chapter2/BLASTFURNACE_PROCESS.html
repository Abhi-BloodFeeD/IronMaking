ESSENTIALS OF BLAST FURNACE PROCESS

    BLAST FURNACE IS A COUNTER CURRENT REACTOR


  • RAW MATERIALS CHARGED FROM TOP , MIXED BY CHUTE
  • HOT AIR BLAST THROUGH TUYERES AT BOTTOM
  • OXYGEN FROM PREHEATED BLAST COMBINES WITH CARBON OF COKE AND GENERATES HEAT AND CARBON MONOXIDE
  • GAS(MAINLY COMPRISING OF CO AND N) RISES UP AND REACTS WITH THE CHARGE
  • THE CHARGE EXTRACT HEAT FROM GASES

MAIN REACTIONS

  1. REDUCTION OF IRON OXIDE TO IRON
    2*Fe2O3 + 3*{CO}= 3*{CO2}+4*Fe
    Indirect reduction
  2. BURNING OF COKE
    Fe2O3+3*{C}= 3*{CO}+2*Fe
    Direct reduction of iron oxide

TUYERE LEVEL


  1. 2*C + {O2} = 2*{CO}
  2. AS CO2 GAS RISE :
    {CO2}+C=2*{CO} solution loss reaction
  3. LIMESTONE AND DOLOMITE , GETS CALCINED
    CaCO2 = CaO + {CO2}
    MgCO3 = MgO + {CO2}
    AT TEMPERATURE ZONE OF 800-1000'C , RESULTING BASIC OXIDES COMBINES WITH GANGUE OXIDES TO FORM SLAG
  • REDUCTION OF IRON PRACTICALLY COMPLETES IN ITS SOLID STATE
  • FINALLY CHARGE DESCENDED TO BOSH REGION MELTS
  • TWO LIQUID STATES,IRON AND SLAG ARE FORMED
  • THOSE OXIDE WHICH DO NOT REDUCE , JOINS THE SLAG
  • SLAG MAINLY COMPRISES OF , SILICA , ALUMINA , ALKALI-ALKALINE EARTH OXIDES E.T.C , WITH OTHER MINOR OXIDES AND SULPHIDES
  • ADDITION OF FLUX IN CHARGE HELPS ,TO A LARGE EXTENT ,IN ACHIEVING THE CORRECT NATURE OF SLAG, I.E LOW VISCOSITY.

PHYSICAL ZONES OF BLAST FURNACE

  • STACK/SHAFT

    1. BEGIN FROM STOCKLIINE DOWN TO MANTLE LEVEL
    2. CHARGE HEATS FROM 200 TO 1100-1200'C IN THIS ZONE
    3. CHARGE MATERIAL EXPANDS PROGRESSIVELY WHILE FALLING WITH RISE IN TEMPERATURE. TO ENSURE FREE FALL CROSS SECTION IS UNIFORMLY INCREASED ALMOST DOUBLE
    4. MUCH OF IRON OXIDE REDUCES IN STACK REGIION

  • BOSH

    1. MELTING OF BURDEN EXCEPT COKE TAKES PLACE HERE
    2. GANGUE AND FLUX COMBINE TO FORM SLAG
    3. WALLS IN THIS REGION EITHER PARALLELL THEN TAPERED OR ENTIRELY TAPERING TO REDUCE SECTIONAL AREA BY 20-25% TO ACCOMODATE RESULTANT DECREASE IN APPARANT VOLUME OF CHARGE.
    4. BURDEN PERMEABILITY IS MAINTAINED BECAUSE OF PRESENCE OF SOLID COKE

  • TUYERE OR COMBUSTION ZONE

    1. OXYGEN OF BLAST BURNS COKE TO CO
    2. NUMBER OF COMBUSTION ZONE, ONE INFRONT OF EVERY TUYER ARE FORMED
    3. IN TUYERE ZONE THERE EXIST RUNWAY OR RACEWAY INFRONT OF EVERY TUYERE
    4. THROUGH RUNWAY FLAMES TUNS AND GAS FLOW SMOOTHLY EXPANDS OVER THE ENTIRE CROSS SECTION OF FURNACE.
    5. TEMPERSTURE IN THIS ZONE IS OFTEN REFFERED AS RACEWAY ADIABETIC FLAME TEMPERATURE (RAFT)

  • HEARTH

    1. ALMOST ALL COKE IS BELIEVED TO BURN AT TUYERE LEVEL
    2. A CERTAIN FRACTION IS BELIEVED TO DESCEND EVEN INTO THE HEARTH WHERE IT IS DISSOLVED IN METAL TO ITS NEAR SATURATION
    3. ENTIRE CHARGE SEPERATES INTO SLAG AND METAL LAYERS IN HEARTH
    4. SLAG AND METAL AS TAPPER SEPARATELY
