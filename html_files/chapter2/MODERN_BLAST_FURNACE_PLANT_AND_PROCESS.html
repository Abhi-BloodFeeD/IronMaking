FURNACE DETAIL

1.HEARTH

It is a receptacle to collect liquid slag and metal and is also refered to as a crucible . Made up of CARBON BLOCKS. Water cooled copper or steel plates in side walls to protect the lining. Tap hole for iron 0.3- 0.6 m above hearth bottom. Slag notch 1.2-1.6 m above iron tap hole.Closed with clay when not in use. At top level of hearth are tuyeres located uniformly.

2.BOSH

Top of the bosh has maximum diameter and is zone of intense heat. It is stadium type construction with steel reinforcement. water cooled copper plates inserted at regular intervals at furnace lining , to protect against high temperature.The intensive cooling of thin walled bosh forms a layer of solidified slag mixed graphite on the lining which in reality protect the inner surfaxe of lining.

3.STACK

Stack is welded steel plate construction lined from inside to a thickness of about a meter of hard fiberbricks.Flat water cooled plates are inserted in the bottom most part of stack lining. Top 2-3 m is protected from abrasion caused by falling charge by providing armour plates on inner surface of lining.

4.TUYERE AND BUSTLE PIPE

Located immediately above the hearth. Hot air blown for fuel combustion.Air from hot blast stove is blown to s huge circulat pipe called bustle pipe. This equialises pressure of blast at all tuyere.

5. BELL AND HOPPER

This system was used for charging the solid charge.It allowed smooth charging without off gas leaking out into open. A modifies bell-less charging with distributing chute known as Paul Wurtz Distributor(BLPWD) replaced traditional double bell. This improved burden distribution inside the furnace and thereby improved efficiency. Still better modification , known as Gimble Top, developed by Siemens Voest Alpine group. C-furnace of Tata Steel adopted it during 2007-2008

6.OFF-TAKE

Four exhaust pipes , connected to furnace top evenly at four points .These rise vertically up and join to a bigger single pipe known as downcomer,which deliver the gas to gas cleaning system,i.e dust catcher

HOT BLAST STOVE

Air blast preheated to temperature of 700-1300`C Set of 3-4 regenerators is provided for each furnace.During working one stove is on blast while other are on gas,i.e getting heated by burning the clean blast furnace gas.

GAS CLEANING

The blast furnace gas is cleaned before being used.The downcomer opens up in dust catcher.Here coarse particles settle down.Coarse cleaned gas is passed through wet or dry scrubbers and electrostatic precipitators.25% gas is used in stove for production of hot preheated blast, 75% gas used elswhere

RAW MATERIAL STORAGE AND HANDLING

To produce one tonne of pig iron , two to three ton of raw materials are required to be charged. A large stock of these raw materials is required to be readily available.

LIQUID PRODUCTS DISPOSAL

Three different products, gas , slag and liquid metal are generated A sufficient large ,raised platform is provided in front of the furnace at a level just below the metal tap hole. Firebrick channels are constructed in platform to allow liqhuid metal and slag seperately to flow into respective ladels,mmoving on rails.A skimmer arrangement seperates slag from metal and diverts it to Slag side. This is calles cast-house Slag and metal both separated. Slag sent to cement plant

LADDLE TYPES

Two type of hot metal laddle for transfering hot metal to steel plant

1.Open-top laddle :
It is open from top,heat loss to atmosphere . The open top laddle is far better for operations such as S or Si or even p removal

2.Torpedo ladle :
This is shaped like torpedo where surface area of hot metal exposed to surface is very small , to conserve heat loss During transfering .

BLAST FURNACE OPERATION AND CONTROL

  1. COKE,LIMESTONE AND ORE ARE NEVER PREMIXED
  2. THESE ARE CHARGED SEPERATELY ONE SKIPFUL AT A TIME
  3. BF OPERATION CONTROLLED FROM TOP BY TRYING TO DISTRIBUTE SOKID CHARGE MORE UNIFORMLY
  4. CONTROLED FROM BOTTOM BY CONTROLING TEMPERATURE AND TUYERE RATE OF BLAST
